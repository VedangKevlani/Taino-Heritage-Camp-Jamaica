What to Expect

Unlike traditional history lessons, THC focuses on activity-based learning, where the past becomes a tool to inspire, nurture, and help students develop different skills. You’ll learn directly in a Taino Yukayeke (village), gaining a new perspective on culture and heritage.

Taino Culture for Modern Living

  • The Taino legacy and what they taught the world
  • Environmental and community responsibility
  • The importance of agriculture
  • Language rejuvenation
  • International, regional, and local exchange

The Taino Experience

Interactive experiences at our traditional Taino yukayeke (village) and conoco (farm). Take part in daily activities and learn first-hand how the Taino slept, ate, and lived by experiencing their food, culture, and lifestyle.

Providing a Home for Taino Heritage

Coki Park is an 18 acre property with 2 acres dedicated to hosting your special events. With the river Eden and its tributaries surrounding the park on three sides we are blessed with a great palm grove, lush botanical gardens, flower farms, and a natural 'clay spa' and bathing pool. From family gatherings to live outdoor concerts, just relax and exhale with the wind at Coki Park The Taino Heritage Camp at Coki Yukayeke is a re-enactment of Pre-Colombian Taino living. We are a registered independent school, creating grassroots programs and workshops in Jamaica for over 20 years. Our workshops are fun, interactive, and fully immersive—built on a philosophy of learning through heritage and nature.

Workshops & Learning

At The Foundation, we respect the importance of learning from our Jamaican Ancestors. Our yukayeke and conoco focus on Jamaica’s Taino and West African heritage, providing descendants with access to ancestral knowledge. All workshops are based on Taino philosophy: working with nature, family, and community.

  • Experience Taino and West African heritage hands-on
  • Interactive cultural activities
  • Sustainable living and community workshops
