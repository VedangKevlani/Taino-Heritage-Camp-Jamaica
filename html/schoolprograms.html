School Field Trips

School field trips to Taino Heritage Camp (THC) are coordinated to be competitive as studies have shown that challenge-based interactive "classrooms" increase student focus, attentiveness, and long-term memory retention.

Understanding the Tainos

THC programs have been created to further Grade 4 and Grade 7 student understanding of their Taino syllabus. Studies have shown that children learn better when they can be a part of the experience being taught.

Interactive Experiences

THC interactive programs have been developed to support the new 2018 Curriculum for the Indigenous People of the Caribbean syllabus. With dance classes, students can feel one with the community and aid in cultural retention.

Proper measures are taken during this activity, and no child is compelled to take part in this activity. With children celebrating the cultural programs offered, they are more likely to retain the information taught during their visit to THC. It is a fun way to unwind, and it is also a great way to bond with their peers and teachers.

Taino-Maroon Unity Walk

An interactive walk of a 600AD Taino yukayeke (village), leading students through various activity points to teach them about traditional Taino living. Activities include:


  • Sports
  • Dance
  • Architecture & Community
  • The Taino and the Maroons
